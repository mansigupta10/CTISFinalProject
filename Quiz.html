Design Tips

One

Riding solo? Well don’t worry! We’ll help make your room your own personal oasis—one where you can escape the everyday struggles of college, study, or simply relax. You’ve got an advantage since you won’t have to worry about conflicts with a roommate. Keep in mind, however, that while you may have more space and privacy, all of the furnishings and essentials will need to be bought by you.

Other notes from the Creators:

You love color and are proud of your school! While you don’t have to overwhelm your room with school spirit, try to incorporate your school colors into some accent pieces to really highlight your pride.
Greenery is a great way to bring your own style into a room! Not only are they great decoration pieces, they help filter the air (which is always a good thing in an older, stuffy dorm building). See if you can find a small plant to put on your desk or windowsill, or if your room is big enough, get a standing plant to put in a corner and add some height to the room!
To keep things tidy and organized, avoid going too crazy with non-essentials. Especially if you spend less time in the room, there’s no need for clutter!
Got wanderlust? Desk and wall decor are great ways to showcase the adventures you’ve taken (or want to take)! Use maps, pictures, and souvenirs to give your room a personalized feel, and it’ll give you a chance to tell people about where you’ve been.
Since dorms are small, make organization your main focus. This can include labels, plastic drawers, and if you want to be trendy, a three-tier rolling cart.
For constant encouragement, print out some of your favorite quotes to hang as reminders.
Staying in can be just as much fun as going out! Invest in a streaming service and some extra seating/pillows for cozy movie nights with your friends.
Even though dorms are small, you still might want to have people over sometimes! Look into some cool folding chairs (like a bungee chair) that can be easily stored behind bigger furniture. Then, pull them out an enjoy movie night!
