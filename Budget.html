1Make a List!

Yes, I know this seems obvious, but the real key to this trick is sticking to your list. If you aren't sure you need to bring, check out our Essentials List to make sure you aren't forgetting anything! Then, as you walk through Target or HomeGoods collecting all of your necessities, make sure you leave that really cute succulent (because we all know you don't need a succulent, no matter how cute it is).

2Can you DIY?

You want your dorm to feel homey, but throw pillows and wall decor add up quickly. While there will be some things that will be nearly impossible to DIY without some serious skill, you'd be surprised how easy it is to sew a throw pillow or make a picture collage for your wall! See what you can do on your own before resorting to store-bought items.

3Talk with your roommate

Especially if you don't already know your roommate personally, it can seem a little awkward to start a conversation, but it will really help you! You don't need two microwaves or two rugs, and these mistakes can be very easily avoided with a quick discussion of who's bringing what.

4Wait until after move in

I know it's easy to get caught up in the excitement of moving out and creating a space from scratch, but it's really easy to overestimate the amount of space available to you. Go ahead and get your essentials, but wait until after you move-in and see what space you have before getting too many extras. And it could be a great activity for you and your new roommate to do together!

5Use your student discount

Another plus of waiting until after you move in, a lot of stores and online retailers offer discounts to students with their student ID. There are a ton of retailers that do this, so do your research and find some goods deals in your area.
